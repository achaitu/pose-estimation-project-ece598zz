University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign

{yulun4, amballa2, sahilb5}@illinois.edu

Abstract

Human pose reconstruction has garnered much interest with the arrival of mainstream Head-mounted (HMD) augmented reality applications. This work focuses on using a diffusion-based framework to estimate human pose from sparse HMD-based positional sensing of a user's body. Earlier works have shown great results by using deep learning for estimating human pose, but unfortunately, nearly all of them are poor at generalizing across multiple users. This is because the positional measurements are heavily influenced by the body shape of the user from whom the data was collected for training. In this project, we perform posterior sampling-based inverse guidance on Diffusion and Flow Matching models to implicitly generalize to all possible user body shapes. Given a user's body shape, our proposed algorithms can generatively estimate the most likely sequence of poses that generated the captured positional signals.
